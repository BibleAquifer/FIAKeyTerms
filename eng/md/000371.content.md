## synagogue ruler (id: 280115)

A **synagogue** is a place where Jewish people meet for prayer and for study of the Scriptures, as well as for other types of discussion and teaching. Each synagogue had a **ruler** who was responsible for the building and for making sure that everything was done properly. The ruler of the synagogue was in charge of inviting people to come forward to pray, to read from the Scriptures, or to teach.

* **Associated Passages:** MRK 5:21–MRK 5:34; MRK 5:35–MRK 5:43
